# Midterm Proposal

## What I Would Like to Do

I’m planning to produce a cinematic electronic track using machine learning and AI tools.

---

## How I Will Do It

| Tools | Focus Areas | Goals |
|-----|-----|-----|
| **Suno** | Generating Ideas & Samples | Using text-to-music prompts or samples from Splice to generate a foundation that I could later build on and edit. |
| **Splice** | Sample & Loop Augmentation | Utilizing AI-powered "Create" modes or "Stacks" to find and sync musical elements. |
| **ACE Studio** | Vocal Synthesis | Creating high-quality vocal tracks using AI singing synthesis. |
| **Logic / Moises / iZotope RX / LALA.AI** | Stem Separation | Taking pieces of generated music and using stem separation so they can be reused as samples. |
| **Logic Master Assistant / Moises / Ozone 12 / Emastered** | Mastering | Applying AI mastering tools to give the final mixed arrangement a polished result. |

---

## Questions, Concerns, and Stretch Goals

My main concern is the quality of generated samples and stem separation tools.

For example, Suno is doing its best to reach the quality of 320 kbps MP3 files, but it's still far from the industry standard of 48kHz / 24-bit WAV files. Stem separation tools also sometimes would generate artifacts, high-frequency noise, or unclear separations where certain sounds are not removed clearly.

My goal is to produce the best possible quality outcome where people would be willing to listen to on streaming platforms just like the other non-AI generated songs.

If necessary, I may only use the generated samples as one-shots instead of loops and polish each sound individually to make them into playable MIDI instruments. I could also use my ears or the audio-to-MIDI functions to recreate and play the instruments.
